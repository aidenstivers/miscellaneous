About Me

My name is Aiden Stivers, and this is my webpage for LIS 636. I am 24-years-old, and I live and work in Louisville, Kentucky. I'm originally from Shelby County, Kentucky, where I grew up on my family's farm. I attended the University of Louisville for my undergraduate degree in Theatre Arts. I focused my studies on performance and props management. I also earned a minor in Creative Writing. I wanted to be a poet for most of my life, but after getting a job at a public library post-grad, I pivoted towards libraries. I also worked at a Starbucks after undergrad, but that was less inspiring. I now work full-time at the University of Louisville Brandeis School of Law as the Circulation Manager in their library. It's a fun job! I get to supervise student workers as well as manage the circulating collection of books, chargers, etc. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, reading, writing, playing games with my friends, picnics, walks, home decor, houseplants, dogs, frogs, poetry, vegetables, thrifting, and my boyfriend. I live with my cat, Thistle, who has anger management problems. I am queer and transgender. My favorite color is green.

Career Goals

My career goal is to work as a teen librarian in a public library. I love teen programming, young adult literature, and connecting with teens and young adults; I found a true passion for public resources and connecting young people with those resources during my time working in a public library. I'm currently working in an special academic library, and I enjoy it, but find I am not as passionate about it as I would like to be. After a few years here, I intend to return to the public library sector.
